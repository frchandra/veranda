> Hello world!

That was the output of the very first computer program I wrote using C before I entered college. At that time, I had
already decided to pursue Information Engineering. I try to recall my state of mind when I made that decision, but now
it is getting harder for me to do so. We sometimes reflect on our journey as we look back on our path. But like walking
on the beach, our tracks have often already been washed away by the waves.

What I remember is: I entered college motivated, went through it ambitious, and graduated with the ideals of an ideal
software developer career path in mind. But when you have become a 社会人, you start to realize that what you went
through in college is just a small portion of what is going on outside.

Now, after several years have passed since I graduated and started building a career, I realize that sticking to the
ideals you had in college is not always the case in the real world. Well, maybe it is actually normal, and many of you
might have already experienced that. But, in addition to the current rapid pace of tech industry development, it could
easily make you feel you are out of the loop.

But somehow, after moving to Japan, I started to see things from a new perspective. The world is never going to stop
spinning. The more you try to catch up, the more tired you will be. Rather than trying to catch the world, you should
catch yourself. Try to learn new things, new languages, new cultures, but at your own pace. As long as the tree is
growing, the fruits will eventually come.

With that being said, I started to think, "Why not preserve the skill acquired from college through small projects? You
have invested much time and money to acquire those skills, isn't it a waste if you just let it go?" Actually, I have
been thinking about creating my own website since college, but because I focused too much on backend development back
then, I never had the time to realize it.

Now, with the current tools and technologies that weren't mature back when I was still in college, learning new things
is never easier. So, I decided to create this website to document my current, past, and future projects, as well as to
share my thoughts and new experiences along the way. I might work at my own pace, but I will try to be consistent.

With all that being said, I hope I can always stay true to myself and continue becoming a better version of myself. That
is all I want to say for now. Thank you.
